Three key insights

This section is the heart of the report: it translates performance into language a campaign, brand, or fundraising lead can act on quickly.

Insight 01

Email outperformed every other channel on conversion efficiency.

Email generated the most donations and the most revenue from a much smaller traffic base. That points to audience trust, message relevance, and stronger intent at the point of visit.

Insight 02

Paid social did its awareness job, but not its full-funnel job.

Paid social delivered reach and visits, yet revenue efficiency lagged behind email and direct traffic. The campaign likely created interest, but did not consistently move that attention into donor-ready action.

Insight 03

Owned and trust-based channels are carrying the real conversion load.

Direct, referral, and email all outperformed many higher-volume sources in terms of conversion value. This suggests the campaign works best when credibility is already established before the visit.

Recommended actions

These are practical next steps, not generic "optimize the funnel" statements.

Rebuild paid social around assisted conversion, not just reach.

Use paid social to qualify audiences into retargeting and email capture journeys, rather than judging success only on traffic volume.

Protect and scale high-trust channels.

Double down on email segmentation, direct reactivation, and partner referral pathways that already show stronger donation efficiency.

Standardize reporting around contribution to revenue.

Move stakeholder reporting away from isolated vanity metrics and toward visits, engaged sessions, donations, and revenue together.
